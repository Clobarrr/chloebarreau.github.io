CONCEPT

I grew up in hong kong, one of the densest cities. I have always been fascinated by the most basic of containers, our skin, and how its compression can simultaneously represent confinement, intimacy and adaptability.

INVESTIGATION OF GROWTHS IN CONFINED SPACES

One night I ate a bowl of pistachios and I imagined making flesh-like folds in the shells.

PRODUCT

This abstract representation of flesh in the womb reflects the movement of the masses, how we are contained, and how our containment increases our tendency to order our environment.

BRADEN STORYTELLING GRANT PODCAST

I am constantly on my screen, as a computer scientist and a visual artist. In many ways I am blind to the physical world because I only see it.

What happens when we block off sight? What reveals itself?

These questions led to me to blind echolocators. I interviewed music researchers, expert echolocators, professors and echolocaton researchers and engineers to learn more about sound. This process made me realize the power of sound in everyday perception- blind or sighted. I recieved a university grant to pursue this investigation right before the summer of 2018 and am currently in the editing the audio and crafting the narrative which will become a 20 minute podcast shared on the Stanford Storytelling Project.
